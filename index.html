MAAV

Pushing autonomous flight forward

MAAV is a fully student led project team at the University of Michigan that builds an autonomous drone to compete at the annual International Aerial Robotics Competition (IARC). Members of MAAV engage in the designing, building, and testing of an autonomous unmanned aerial system (UAS) while furthering multiple areas of Computer Science and Aerospace Engineering research.



This is what we do

Structures


Our structures team is responsible for the design and fabrication of MAAV's airframes. Our quadcopter is a combination of carbon fiber, milled plastic, and 3D printed plastic, which creates a durable, light airframe that can withstand intense maneuvers and crashes.

Software


Our software team develops and implements algorithms that enable the drone to fly on its own, and creates utilities, such as simulators, that are used by other subteams. We also write algorithms to process raw data collected from our sensors.

Circuits


Circuits designs and builds electrical hardware, for both the vehicle and for testing. The vehicle's printed circuit board (PCB) provides a platform for the control software to run and communicate with the navigation software, as well as manage the safety of the battery, motors, and operators.

Business


Our business team is responsible for outreach and management. We contact potential sponsors, create the annual budget for all the teams, and manage the organization to ensure all teams are working in unison to develop and improve the drone.
